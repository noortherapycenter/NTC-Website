Level II Provider

Behavior therapist. Delivers EIDBI intervention, participates in family and caregiver training, coordinated care conferences, and ITP implementation — under the supervision of a QSP.

Qualification Requirements (DHS-7120E)

Bachelor's degree pathway

Bachelor's degree in behavioral health, child development, or a related field, plus at least one of:

  • 1,000 hours of supervised clinical experience or training with ASD or related conditions (or equivalent graduate coursework)
  • BCaBA certification
  • Registered Behavior Technician (RBT) certification
  • Certification in another DHS-recognized treatment modality
  • QSP or Level I observation & direction at least twice per month until reaching 1,000 supervised hours

Non-bachelor's pathways — at least one

  • Currently enrolled in a qualifying bachelor's program, with twice-monthly observation & direction until 1,000 supervised hours
  • Associate's degree in a related field plus 2,000 hours of supervised clinical experience with ASD or related conditions
  • 4,000 hours of supervised clinical experience delivering treatment to people with ASD or related conditions
  • Graduate student in a related field receiving clinical supervision from an agency QSP
  • 18+, fluent in a non-English language or tribally certified, Level III training complete, with weekly observation & direction until 1,000 hours
  • 18+, initial certification in the agency's treatment modality plus Level III training, with weekly observation & direction until 1,000 hours
