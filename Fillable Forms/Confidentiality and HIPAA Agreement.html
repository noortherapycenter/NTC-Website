Confidentiality & HIPAA Agreement

All employees, contractors, interns, and volunteers must sign before any access to client information.

1

What Is Protected

In the course of your work you will encounter protected health information (PHI) — anything that identifies a client or relates to their health, treatment, or payment: names, faces, schedules, diagnoses, session notes, data sheets, billing records, and even the fact that a person receives services here. You will also encounter confidential business information such as employee records, rates, and internal procedures. All of it is covered by this agreement, whether spoken, written, or electronic.

2

My Commitments

  • I will access, use, and share PHI only as needed to do my job — the minimum necessary for the task.
  • I will not discuss clients in waiting areas, hallways, public places, or with anyone not involved in their care.
  • I will not photograph, record, or post about clients or the workplace on social media, and I will follow the Photography & Media Consent on file for each client.
  • I will keep documents secured, lock screens when stepping away, use only agency-approved systems for PHI, and never send PHI to personal email or devices.
  • I will not share passwords or use another person's login.
  • I will report any suspected privacy breach, lost device, or misdirected PHI to my supervisor or the Privacy Officer immediately.
  • I understand these obligations continue after my employment ends, and that on separation I will return all client and Company information in my possession.
3

Consequences

Violations of this agreement or of HIPAA and Minnesota privacy laws (including the Minnesota Health Records Act) may result in discipline up to and including termination, and can carry personal civil and criminal penalties. Reporting suspected maltreatment of a minor or vulnerable adult to the proper authorities is required by law and is not a breach of this agreement.
