Notice of Privacy Practices

This notice describes how medical information about your child may be used and disclosed and how you can get access to this information. Please review it carefully.

1

How We May Use and Disclose Health Information

Treatment

We use your child's health information to provide, coordinate, and manage therapy services — for example, sharing evaluation results and treatment data among the QSP and treatment providers on your child's team, or with other health care providers involved in your child's care.

Payment

We may use and disclose health information to bill and collect payment — for example, submitting claims and authorization requests to Minnesota Health Care Programs or your managed-care plan, verifying coverage, and responding to payer audits.

Health Care Operations

We may use health information for our own operations — quality reviews, clinical supervision, training, audits, and compliance activities.

Uses and Disclosures Required or Permitted by Law

  • Required by law: reporting suspected maltreatment of minors or vulnerable adults, responding to court orders, and disclosures to the Minnesota Department of Human Services or other oversight agencies.
  • Health and safety: to prevent a serious threat to the health or safety of your child or others, or in an emergency.
  • Family and caregivers: with people involved in your child's care, to the extent you agree or do not object.

Most other uses and disclosures — including marketing, sale of information, and most sharing of psychotherapy-type notes — require your written authorization, which you may revoke at any time in writing.

2

Your Rights

  • Inspect and copy your child's record (we may charge a reasonable copying fee).
  • Request amendment of information you believe is incorrect or incomplete.
  • Request restrictions on certain uses and disclosures (we are not always required to agree).
  • Request confidential communications — for example, contact at an alternate phone or address.
  • An accounting of disclosures we have made for purposes other than treatment, payment, and operations.
  • A paper copy of this notice at any time, even if you agreed to receive it electronically.
3

Our Duties & Complaints

We are required by law to keep your child's health information private, provide you this notice, follow its terms, and notify you if a breach compromises your child's information. We may update this notice; the current version is always available at our front desk and will be posted at the center.

If you believe your privacy rights have been violated, you may file a complaint with us at the address above or with the U.S. Department of Health and Human Services, Office for Civil Rights. You will not be penalized or retaliated against for filing a complaint.
